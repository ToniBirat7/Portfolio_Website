SEO + Monetization Playbook for the Blog

## 1. Distribution

* Share every new post across Twitter/X, LinkedIn, and Reddit. Automate this so publishing one markdown file can trigger a consistent social push.
* Cross-post selective articles to Medium or Dev.to when the topic is broadly useful. Use canonical links or a clear reference back to the original post to avoid splitting SEO value.
* Add a newsletter flow that emails subscribers whenever a new post goes live. This keeps repeat traffic alive instead of depending only on search.

## 2. SEO Foundations

* Dynamically update metadata for each post: title, description, keywords, canonical URL, and social preview tags.
* Use the blog content itself to generate focused meta descriptions and keyword themes, instead of writing generic metadata by hand.
* Track page views, scroll depth, and click-through rates with analytics so the content strategy can be adjusted using real behavior.
* Build automation around the markdown files if possible. A bot or workflow can suggest improvements, generate metadata, identify missing links, and flag weak posts.
* If we use `LangChain` or another agentic workflow, keep it narrow: content review, SEO suggestions, and topic discovery are useful; do not let it rewrite the whole site blindly.

## 3. Monetization Strategy

* Use Google AdSense and affiliate links as the first monetization layer.
* Prioritize high-CPC categories when deciding future blog topics. The strongest global categories are usually:
	* Legal Services
	* Insurance
	* Finance & Investing
	* Marketing & Advertising
	* Technology and SaaS
* For Nepal traffic, the better-performing categories tend to be:
	* Employment and Recruitment
	* Finance and Banking
	* Business and Industrial services
	* Real Estate
	* Education and EdTech

## 4. Audience Targeting

* Optimize for international traffic, not just local traffic. Tier 1 audiences such as the US, UK, Canada, and Australia usually have far better CPC and RPM than Nepal-only traffic.
* Publish around globally searchable problems, technical decisions, and business topics that attract readers outside Nepal.
* Keep mobile performance tight. Most Nepali users browse on phones, and fast pages help both engagement and ad viewability.
* Treat page views as only one part of the equation. A smaller audience from a high-value country can outperform a much larger local audience.

## 5. Practical Direction

* Use globally relevant topics for the main content plan.
* Keep a smaller set of Nepal-relevant high-value posts for local monetization.
* Make SEO metadata and internal linking part of the publishing workflow.
* Measure what brings traffic from international markets, then double down on those themes.

## 6. Key Takeaway

* If the goal is stronger AdSense revenue, the blog should be built around high-intent topics, international search demand, and repeatable promotion.
* Nepal traffic alone can monetize, but it is usually not enough to rely on unless the site reaches very high volume.
* The best long-term strategy is to combine global topics, strong SEO, and consistent distribution.
